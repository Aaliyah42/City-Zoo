ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the world’s largest land animal! Male African elephants can reach 3m tall and weigh between 4,000 -7,500kg. Asian elephants are slightly smaller, reaching 2.7m tall and weighing 3,000– 6,000kg.

Elephants are herbivores and spend up to 16 hours a day eating. They can consume up to 150kg of food a day, including grass, leaves, bark, roots and fruit.

Elephants are highly intelligent and social animals. They live in family groups led by a matriarch, usually

There’s an easy way to tell the African elephants apart from their Asian cousins – their ears! African elephants have large ears shaped like the continent of Africa! Asian elephants’ ears are smaller and shaped like India. Cool, huh?

Elephants are known for their incredible memories. They can remember locations, other elephants, and even human faces for many years.

Elephants communicate using a variety of sounds, including trumpets, rumbles, and even infrasound, which is below the range of human hearing.

Elephants are also known for their strong social bonds. They often show affection by touching and hugging each other with their trunks.

Sadly, elephants are threatened by habitat loss and poaching for their ivory tusks. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these magnificent creatures.
